User Research in UI/UX

What is User Research?

User research is the process of understanding your users' needs, behaviors, motivations, and pain points. It helps designers build user-centered products.

It involves asking questions, observing actions, and analyzing user feedback to make design decisions backed by real data.

Why is it Important?

  • Reduces guesswork in design
  • Increases product usability
  • Reveals real user problems
  • Improves user satisfaction

Types of User Research

  • Qualitative: Observational and subjective (e.g. interviews, usability tests)
  • Quantitative: Numerical and data-driven (e.g. surveys, analytics)

Common User Research Methods

  • User Interviews
  • Surveys & Questionnaires
  • Usability Testing
  • Field Studies
  • Heatmaps & Analytics

Popular Tools for UX Research

  • Hotjar
  • Google Forms / Typeform
  • UserTesting
  • Maze
  • Figma + Plugins (like FigJam polls)
