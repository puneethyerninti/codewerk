Wireframes & Prototypes

What is a Wireframe?

A wireframe is a basic layout or sketch of a digital product's interface. It shows structure and layout without detailed visuals or colors. Think of it as a blueprint for your website or app.

Wireframes help designers and stakeholders understand the flow, content placement, and navigation early in the design process.

Low-Fidelity vs High-Fidelity

  • Low-Fidelity: Simple sketches or grayscale blocks to represent elements.
  • High-Fidelity: Detailed layouts that include typography, colors, and UI components.

What is a Prototype?

A prototype is a working model that simulates how users will interact with the final product. It can include clickable buttons, transitions, and animations.

Prototypes are great for testing usability and collecting feedback before actual development starts.
